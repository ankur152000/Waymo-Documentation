This guide helps diagnose and resolve Common Hardware, Desktop Application, and Driver Issues with the Alio One Axis and Three-Axis ACS Controller.

Types of Malfunction and TroubleShooting Steps:

Type of fault Examples Possible Troubleshooting Steps
User error The power adapter not connected or is faulty. Verify the connection
Improper or broken wiring A loose connection in the feedback encoder wiring. 1.Inspect and secure all wiring connections.
2.Use an oscilloscope or multimeter to check for continuity and signal integrity.
Power amplifier malfunction The power amplifier malfunctions and sends a fault signal to the controller 1.Check for amplifier fault codes in the ACS software.
2.Inspect power connections and ensure proper grounding.
3.Reset the amplifier and reinitialize the controller.
3.Check the controller logs for specific error messages.
Motor malfunction A motor overheats. 1.Verify motor load and operating conditions.
2.Check motor cooling and ventilation.
3.Ensure proper current limits and thermal protection settings.
4.Inspect and clean motor connections.
Controller hardware malfunctions The Main Processor Unit (MPU) and the Servo Processors (SPs) work together to detect malfunctions in the controller. Examples include the servo processor alarm and the servo interrupt. 1.Perform a power cycle reset on the controller.
2.Check for firmware updates or reflash the firmware if necessary.
3.Run controller self-diagnostics using ACS tools.
4. Contact ACS technical support if the issue persists.

Note:How the Controller Detects Malfunctions

  1. To detect malfunctions the controller monitors safety inputs, such as limit switches, and internal safety conditions, such as comparing the reference position with the software limits.
  2. Internal safety conditions may consist of a static formula, such as checking the acceleration limit, or may include time dependencies, such as measuring the time interval between two interrupts to detect servo interrupt faults.
  3. Some safety conditions are a mixture of both techniques. For example, position error control is based on a static condition, whether a motor is positioned at a certain location, and a time dependency, how long the motor is positioned at the location.
